CryptoCrystal Metrics

Crypto Crystals, deployed in May 2018, was one of the first Ethereum games. Players can collect and merge crystals of different weights and rarities. Use our app to help identify undervalued stones! We will add features to extend the usefulness of the application based on user feedback; find @amphiboly or @ro4438 in the Crypto Crystals Discord.

FAQ

CryptoCrystals are sentient Shiny Crystals that have personalities similar to humans while being overly cute like pet animals. Each of them is unique on Ethereum, with no duplicated DNA structure. Players can enjoy mining, collecting, and trading mysterious living minerals that live inside the Blockchain. CryptoCrystals is not a CryptoCurrency, but rather a CryptoCollectible. CryptoCrystals has no management operator apart from the Smart Contract. Every definition in the smart contract will be made public. No one, not even the developer, can change the rarity rates or other aspects of the contract.


Fun fact: Devin Finzer, Co-Founder of OpenSea, actually approached CryptoCrystals to list them on their new NFT platform as they did not have many projects on there at the time. Crypto Crystals was listed on May 18, 2018, one of the first NFT projects on OpenSea.

CryptoCrystals was created as a subproject of Quan Inc, a character/sticker company based in Tokyo with over 4.5 billion global downloads. Eventually, the project was handed over to the Product Lead, Minicoohei, who is a Japanese influencer on Ethereum and the founder of Japan’s biggest Ethereum wallet: tokenPocket. Minicoohei is also an advisor for Double Jump Tokyo and MyCryptoheroes.

There are 100 kinds of shiny crystals. The total supply of Crystals is not restricted and you can technically still mine Crystals through the smart contract, but it has become increasingly unprofitable to do so due to diminishing weights. Crystals can be burned (melted) to form larger crystals. Weight and type of crystals are decided at the time of mining, and are predicated on the total reserves of each type present at that time. These identifiers, such as name, weight, and type, are written in the blockchain and are immutable.

These metrics are designed to provide various information and insights about the crystal types to assist in the buying and selling of CryptoCrystals. By calculating the intrinsic value of each type based on a variety of factors, users will gain a better understanding of the feasibility of CryptoCrystal transactions. You can hover on column names for more information/descriptions about the individual metrics. We also plan on adding an update that will recommend good deals for those interested in purchasing a crystal.
